Grammar: passive voice

What is passive voice? Why do we need to use it? How do we form it?

Passive voice (umbisikuline tegumood) is used when we don't know or don't care who performs an action, or when it is obvious who the actor is.

Passive voice is called 'passive' because the subject of the sentence is passive.

For example: A tree was cut down - the tree itself did nothing, it was passive; someone else cut down the tree, but we don't know or don't care who it was.

So the emphasis is on the action itself and the actor is not important or not known.
