Grammar: future tenses

How do we talk about the future?

There are several future tenses in English, but we also use present tenses to talk about the future. In the following pages we will take a look at:
  • Future simple - Next year will be better than this! (To talk about predictions, instant decisions and future facts)
  • Future continuous - At 5 pm I will be cooking dinner. (To talk about events that will be in progress at a certain time in the future)
  • Present continuous - I am spending my holidays in Greece. (To talk about fixed future arrangements)
  • 'Going to' future - I am going to travel to Spain next year. (To talk about future plans and intentions)
  • Present simple - The train leaves at 5. (To talk about scheduled events)
If you feel confident enough using these tenses, you can scroll down, watch the review video and do the practice exercises.

Future simple
How and when do we use the future simple?

Estonians tend to overuse the future simple, so be careful with this tense!

We use the future simple in the following cases:
  • to make predictions 
  • to talk about decisions we have just made
  • to talk about facts
  • to make promises, offers or suggestions
Please note that the future simple is NOT used to talk about
  • future arrangements
  • future plans
